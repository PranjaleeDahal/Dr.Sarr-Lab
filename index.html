About Dr. Sarr

Dr. Sait Sarr received his Bachelor of Science degree in Agriculture, Food, and the Environment, a Master of Science degree in Environmental Studies, and a Master of Business Administration degree, all from Kentucky State University (KSU) in Frankfort, KY. His master's thesis focused on sustainable agricultural practices that are both beneficial to farmers while protecting the environment. He earned his Doctoral degree from the University of Louisville with a focus on Urban Sustainability. He is currently a Senior Research Scientist and Adjunct Faculty in the College of Agriculture, health, and Natural Resources and the College of Business, Engineering, and Technology at Kentucky State University teaching courses in Agriculture, Food, Environment, Business, Management, and Economics. He has published articles in different journals and made several paper presentations at professional conferences. His research interests include urban farming, pollution management, community sustainability and development, the development and effectiveness of environmental and energy policies, environmental justice, and poverty eradication. He is currently the Principal Investigator of a United States Department of Agriculture-National Institute of Food and Agriculture Capacity Building Grant (USDA Award # 2025-38821-45463) providing knowledge and skills to K-12 school youths, KSU students, faculty, and staff, local growers, and other stakeholders in innovative local food production systems and marketing strategies.

Meet the Team

Eric Murwanashyaka is a Research and Extension Associate at Kentucky State University with a passion for sustainable agriculture, urban food systems, digital agriculture, and community education. Originally from Rwanda, he earned a bachelor’s degree in Agronomy from EARTH University in Costa Rica and later completed a master’s degree in environmental studies with a specialization in Controlled Environment Agriculture at Kentucky State University. His experience spans hydroponics systems, soil science research, sustainable food production, agricultural education, and community outreach. In addition to his agricultural background, Eric has developed skills in DevOps, cloud computing, Linux systems, and digital agriculture technologies, with a particular interest in integrating data-driven tools and automation into food production systems. He works with youth and communities in Kentucky to establish community gardens and hydroponic systems, teaching practical skills that improve food security, promote local food production, and build healthier, more resilient communities.

Subas Thapa Magar is a Research and Extension Assistant for the USDA-funded project, “Enhancing Urban Agricultural Research, Education, and Extension Opportunities for Students and Staff of Kentucky State University.” His work focuses on sustainable agriculture research, field demonstrations, and extension programming. He supports vegetable production through raised bed construction, soil preparation, irrigation, hydroponic system installation, greenhouse setup, and sustainable crop management. He also collaborates with community members and middle and high school students to establish and maintain community and school gardens while providing hands-on training in sustainable vegetable production. He contributes to advancing local food production, food security, and community engagement through research, education and outreach.
